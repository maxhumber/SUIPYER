Advertisement

Shadows of Brimstone: City of the Ancients is a fast-paced, fully cooperative, dungeon-crawl board game set in the Old West, with a heavy dose of unspeakable horror! Players create characters, taking on the role of a classic Western Hero Archetype, such as the Law Man, Gunslinger, or Saloon Girl. Forming an adventuring posse, the Heroes venture down into the dark mines, overrun with all manner of ancient demons and foul creatures from another world. With tactical gameplay, lots of dice, and a robust card-driven exploration system, no two games are ever the same as the heroes explore the mines finding new enemies to fight, new loot to collect, and new dangers to overcome. Players can even find portals to other worlds, stepping through to continue their adventures on the other side!

An exciting campaign system allows the players to visit local frontier towns between adventures, spending their hard-earned loot and building their characters from game to game! As players find fantastic gear and artifacts to equip their heroes, they also gain experience from their adventures. This experience is used to level up, guiding the hero's path through an expansive, class-specific upgrade tree of new skills and abilities, allowing each player to develop their hero to fit their own play style.

In City of the Ancients, players will encounter portals to the otherworld on the Plains of Targa, finding an ancient frozen city whose living inhabitants are nowhere to be found. Instead, great mechanical keepers wander the city going about their duties - until interrupted by the arrival of the players' characters!

So load up yer' six shooter, throw on yer' hat and poncho, and gather the posse as the darkness is coming, and all hell's about to break loose...in the Shadows of Brimstone!

Can be used together with Shadows of Brimstone: Swamps of Death to raise the maximum players to 6.

FAQ & Errata

The Official FAQ is available here.

Below are links to BGG threads in which the designer has answered questions.

CHARACTERS

GUNSLINGER

Q - Previously using Cool Hand brought your initiative from 6 to 3 but what happens post errata? QuickDraw now makes your initiative 10 the first round of a fight. If I use Cool Hand' the first round of the fight does my initiative:
1.) still go to 3
2.) does it go to 7 (6-3=3)
3.) does it go to 5 (6/2=3)
A - The Cool Hand ability says, "At the start of any Fight Turn, you may reduce your Initiative by 3 to..."

If this is used in the first turn of a Fight with the revised version of Quickdraw, it would make your Initiative 7 (10-3=7). I think you may have been reading Cool Hand as reducing Initiative to 3 rather than reducing it by 3. Link to Answer

Q - What happens when your Gunslinger has a native Initiative of 10+?
A - The revised card text includes "Initiative 10 (unless already higher)". If your Gunslinger's Initiative is already 10 or higher though, the Quickdraw ability will not be as much of a benefit, just giving you the +1 Shot. Link to Answer

Gunslinger Quickdraw Ability got a bit of an overhaul to keep the thematic flavor but tone it down a bit and make it easier to use/understand. The old version is gone and the new version should read, "In the first turn of a Fight, you are Initiative 10 and gain +1 Shot with a <single hand> Gun". Link

SALOON GIRL
Saloon Girl Comforting Presence should say, "At the end of the turn..." rather than at the end of the 'Hero turn'. This was a terminology change that slipped through. Link

Note that there is a correction on the Saloon Girl Pickpocket Event at the Saloon. It should be if she rolls one or more 6s, she gets a single Gear (not a Gear for every 6 rolled). Link

RANCHER
Rancher Rapid Shot ability is now limited to a max of +3 extra Shots per turn. Link

ENEMIES

UNDEAD GUNSLINGER
High Threat card states add 6 undead outlaws and a Medium threat card. The graphic indicates a Low threat card. The text is correct, a Medium threat card should be drawn.

LOCATIONS

TOWN

Q - Can Curses be removed at the regular church in Town or only at the Church Tent in the Camp. How about Parasites and the Doc's Office vs the Doc's Tent?
A - The intent is that it is only available at the Camp Site Doc's/Church Tent because going to the Camp site is more dangerous and, thematically, because dealing with curses and parasites is more fringe and not something a regular Doc or Church would know how to handle. Link to Answer

Q - What are the official rules for moving Enemies off the roof of a building?

The book says that they use their Attack to move ON to the roof, but there isn't any mention of an enemy moving back down stairs to pursue a targeted Hero who either escaped or fell off. Do non-ranged enemies also use up their Attack getting back down, and then do their normal move afterward?
A - An Enemy on a Rooftop that targets a Hero on the ground for a Melee Attack will give up its Attack to move to the space in the Building below that is closest (along the shortest viable movement path) to its intended target, and then continue its move to try and get adjacent to that target Hero. This is basically the reverse of a Melee Enemy getting up onto a Rooftop. Link to Answer

Q - Does Darkstone Bullets (or other ammo besides the special Shotgun Ammo) affect Sawed-Off Shotgun splash damage?
A - Splash Damage for the Sawed-Off Shotgun is not intended to be a Hit per se, but rather a by-product of the initial Hit. The Splash Damage itself is not intended to gain modifiers, but rather to simply be a single extra Wound, ignoring Defense, on every Enemy model adjacent to the target.

Dark Stone Bullets give you +1 Damage on all of your Gun Hits, but should not add to the Splash Damage of the Sawed-off Shotgun.

Inferno Shots at the Smuggler's Den are +1 Damage on all Shotgun Critical Hits, and in addition, specifically give a +1 Damage on the Splash Damage of the Sawed-Off Shotgun as well. Link to Answer

Q - Do I still use the original game's camp site hazard chart?
A - Yes

Q - Do I still use the original game's town event chart? (Days since last event roll)
A - Yes

Q - Lets say I am drawing a card for a frontier town event and I get "Market Prices Down: All prices in town today are -$10 to a min of 10". I further go to the general store and get the location event Fire sale "All purchase prices are reduced by -$50 to a min of $25." What do I do? And for that matter, what if I get a card that drops prices but a location event that raises prices together?
A - These events are not mutually exclusive. The total discount or cost increase would be the sum of all applicable modifiers. Take the bigger discount first, down to its minimum, then take the smaller discount off that, down to its minimum Link to Answer

WEAPONS

The Trusty Pistol is meant to have a 'Frontier Heroes Only' tag on it. This also slipped through on the version that went to print. The upcoming Drifter Hero Class starts with a Trusty Pistol, and the Starting Gear version that comes with him has this correction on it. Link

MISSIONS

LAST STAND - The text under the Objective 'Defeating Enough Enemies' reads, "For every full 3 Enemies the Heroes Kill..." and "For each Large or bigger Enemy...".

The intent is that the Hero Posse marker moves 1 space for every full set of 3 Medium or smaller Enemies, and 2 spaces for each Large or bigger Enemy. Link
